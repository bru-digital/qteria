> **Generated**: November 2025
> **Product**: Qteria - AI Document Pre-Assessment Platform
> **Status**: Final
> **Planning Horizon**: 24 months (2026-2027)

## Overview

This document defines the product strategy for Qteria, an AI-driven document pre-assessment platform for the TIC (Testing, Inspection, Certification) industry. This strategy validates the user journey (product-guidelines/00-user-journey.md) with market reality and establishes the foundation for sustainable growth.

**Strategic Intent**: Build a profitable, sustainable SaaS business serving TIC notified bodies with exceptional UX, rock-solid reliability, and white-glove support that enables family wealth creation.

## How This Traces to User Journey

**Journey audience** (from `product-guidelines/00-user-journey.md`):
- Project Handlers at TIC notified bodies running 15-20 assessments/day
- Process Managers creating validation workflows for certification types

**Journey problem** (from `product-guidelines/00-user-journey.md`):
- 1-2 day wait for India outsourcing teams ($100K/year)
- Quality anxiety from errors slipping through to Certification Person
- Hard to experiment with new validation criteria

**Journey solution** (from `product-guidelines/00-user-journey.md`):
- AI validation in <10 minutes with evidence-based results
- Simple workflow creation (buckets + criteria)
- Iterative refinement of validation rules

**Strategy Goal**:
- Validate that TIC notified bodies represent viable beachhead market
- Confirm document pre-assessment pain is significant enough to pay $30K/year
- Differentiate through radical simplicity + data privacy + white-glove support
- Project sustainable path to $500K-1M ARR within 3 years

## Market Analysis

### Market Size

**TAM (Total Addressable Market)**:
- **Definition**: All organizations that pre-assess documents against compliance criteria globally
- **Size**: ~$10M/year
- **Calculation**:
  - ~100 TIC notified bodies globally (EU + US + Asia) × $30K = $3M
  - ~200-300 MedTech consulting/regulatory firms × $30K = $6-9M
  - Other regulated industries (pharma, automotive, aerospace): TBD
- **Note**: Conservative estimate focused on beachhead; actual TAM likely larger

**SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**:
- **Definition**: Large TIC notified bodies in Europe reachable via direct sales
- **Size**: ~$600-900K/year
- **Calculation**:
  - 20-30 large notified bodies in Europe (TÜV SÜD, BSI, TÜV Rheinland, DEKRA, etc.)
  - At $30K/year average contract value
- **Rationale**: Focus on Europe first (most notified bodies concentrated here), large bodies first (budget + pain)

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**:
- **Definition**: Realistic revenue capture in 3 years
- **Size**: ~$300K ARR by Year 3
- **Calculation**:
  - Year 1 (2026): 1 customer (TÜV SÜD) = $30K ARR
  - Year 2 (2027): 4 customers total = $120K ARR
  - Year 3 (2028): 10 customers total = $300K ARR
- **Assumptions**: 1 new customer per quarter after TÜV SÜD proof of concept

### Market Dynamics

**Growth rate**:
- Document compliance market growing at ~8-12% annually (regulatory complexity increasing)
- AI adoption in enterprise accelerating post-2023 (ChatGPT moment)
- TIC industry slow to adopt new tech, but cost pressure driving change

**Key trends**:
1. **AI mainstreaming in enterprise**: Post-2023, executives expect AI solutions. "Why are we still using India teams when AI can do this?" becomes a boardroom question.
2. **Data privacy regulations tightening**: GDPR, ISO 27001, SOC2 becoming table-stakes. Notified bodies can't risk data leaks to third-party AI vendors.
3. **Certification pipeline pressure**: Medical device approvals, product certifications taking longer. Industry pressure to speed up without compromising quality.

**Market forces**:
- **Enablers**:
  - LLM capability maturation (GPT-4, Claude 3.5 can read PDFs accurately)
  - Enterprise AI adoption mindset shift
  - Cost pressure on notified bodies ($100K/year for basic checks is hard to justify)
  - Founder's insider access to TÜV SÜD (beachhead customer ready)

- **Barriers**:
  - Conservative industry (slow to trust new tech)
  - Data privacy concerns (confidential documents, regulatory scrutiny)
  - Switching costs (retraining Process Managers on new tool)
  - Incumbent inertia ("India team works fine, why change?")

## Product Positioning

### Positioning Statement

**For** TIC notified bodies and certification organizations
**Who** spend $100K/year and wait 1-2 days for basic document pre-assessment from outsourced teams
**Qteria** is an AI-powered compliance validation platform
**That** delivers evidence-based pass/fail results in <10 minutes with radical simplicity and enterprise-grade data privacy
**Unlike** complicated AI document tools with feature creep or slow human outsourcing teams
**Our product** focuses exclusively on validation workflows (buckets + criteria), provides exact evidence (page/section links), and includes white-glove support with dedicated relationship managers

### Differentiation

**What makes us different**:

1. **Radical Simplicity**: ONLY workflow → buckets → criteria → validate. No feature bloat. Process Managers create workflows in <30 minutes vs. hours with competitors.

2. **Evidence-Based Validation**: Every AI result links to exact document location (page, section). No black-box "AI says no" - always shows proof.

3. **Enterprise Data Privacy**: Zero-retention AI policies, SOC2/ISO 27001 compliance, private deployment options. Notified bodies trust us with confidential certification documents.

4. **White-Glove Support**: Dedicated relationship manager per customer. Not a support ticket - a named person who knows your workflows.

5. **Expertise Scaling**: Validation workflows are shareable, refineable assets. Process Managers experiment and improve criteria over time (future: monetize via marketplace).

**Why it's defensible**:
- Simplicity is a hard discipline (competitors add features, we subtract)
- Evidence-based AI requires specialized engineering (not just API wrapper)
- White-glove service doesn't scale to thousands of customers (but perfect for 20-50 enterprise accounts)
- Founder's TIC industry insider knowledge (understands notified body workflows deeply)

## Product Principles

**Decision-making framework for product choices**

### Principle 1: Simplicity Over Features

**What it means**:
Ship minimal, focused tools. Resist feature creep. If it doesn't directly serve the core flow (workflow → buckets → criteria → validate → evidence), cut it. Every new feature request is a "no" by default.

**Example**:
User asks for "batch processing 50 assessments at once". Answer: No - that adds complexity. Focus on making single assessment flow perfect first. Maybe in Year 3.

**Trade-offs**:
We lose customers who want "everything in one platform." But we win customers who are drowning in complicated tools and want something that "just works."

---

### Principle 2: Data Privacy is Non-Negotiable

**What it means**:
Customer data never trains our models, never leaves their control (or only with zero-retention guarantees), full encryption at rest and in transit, complete audit trails. Pass SOC2/ISO 27001 from day one.

**Example**:
If OpenAI API doesn't offer zero-retention agreement, we use Anthropic (who does) or self-host open-source models. Never compromise on data privacy for convenience.

**Trade-offs**:
Higher infrastructure costs (private deployments, encryption overhead). Slower AI iteration (can't use customer data to fine-tune models). But this is the price of entry for notified bodies - non-negotiable.

---

### Principle 3: Evidence Over Opinion

**What it means**:
Every AI validation MUST show evidence - page number, section reference, direct link to document location. No black-box "AI says fail" without proof. Users trust what they can verify.

**Example**:
Result screen shows: "Criteria 2: Test summary missing - FAIL → [Link: test-report.pdf, page 8, section 3.2]". User clicks link, sees exact location, understands why AI flagged it.

**Trade-offs**:
Harder AI engineering (need document parsing, section detection, precise linking). But this builds trust - users can verify AI is correct, not guessing.

---

### Principle 4: White-Glove Support

**What it means**:
Dedicated relationship manager per customer. Every notified body has a named person they can call/email directly. Fast, personal support. Not a support ticket queue.

**Example**:
TÜV SÜD's relationship manager is [Founder]. They have founder's direct email/phone. When they have a question, they text/call, get answer same day. Not "ticket #12345, we'll get back to you in 3 business days."

**Trade-offs**:
Doesn't scale to thousands of customers (founder can manage 10-20 relationships max). But perfect for enterprise B2B with 20-50 high-value customers at $30K/year each.

---

### Principle 5: Ship Working Software

**What it means**:
Reliability over speed. Don't ship broken features. Test thoroughly before launch. Reputation for "it just works" is earned by never shipping buggy code.

**Example**:
If AI validation accuracy is <90%, don't launch publicly. Delay 2 weeks, improve model, hit 95%+, then launch. Users remember broken experiences forever.

**Trade-offs**:
Slower iteration speed vs. competitors who "move fast and break things." But in regulated industries (TIC, MedTech), broken software = lost trust = lost customers.

   - **Assumption**: Founder's insider relationship + their stated interest in AI automation = contract by Q2 2026

   - **Assumption**: Can use AI APIs with zero-retention agreements OR self-host open-source models within MVP budget
   - **Validation plan**: Negotiate with OpenAI/Anthropic for enterprise privacy terms, evaluate self-hosted alternatives (Llama, Mistral)
   - **If wrong**: May need higher funding to build private AI infrastructure ($50-100K+), or lose enterprise customers who can't accept API risk

4. **$30K/year pricing is acceptable**
   - **Assumption**: Notified bodies paying $100K/year for India teams will pay $30K/year for AI (70% savings)

   - **If wrong**: May need to lower price ($15-20K) or raise it ($50K) based on value perception - affects unit economics

   - **Assumption**: Founder can manage 10-20 customer relationships personally while building product
   - **Validation plan**: Track time spent on customer support vs. product development, evaluate at 5 customers
   - **If wrong**: May need to hire customer success manager earlier than planned, or reduce support intensity (loses differentiation)
